My Story

Hannah Webb was born in El Segundo, CA and spent her early years out west. After leaving California, her family spent some time in Wenatchee, Washington and Spanish Fork, Utah. The Webb family eventually made their way to where Hannah now calls home: Medina, Ohio.

Growing up, Hannah spent most of her time playing music and sports. She loved to learn new instruments (piano, violin, clarinet, guitar) and participate in many extra-curriculars (soccer, basketball, track & field, yearbook staff). Surrounded by loving parents and siblings, she always felt blessed with a great support system.

Professionally, Hannah's work experience began when she started her own at-home piano studio as her very first job. Upon graduating high school, Hannah worked at BYU Alumni Aspen Grove Family Camp for two summers. Eighteen months later, after returning from a volunteer mission trip in Tucson, Arizona for her church, Hannah spent a summer in a warehouse at 1st Day School Supplies.

Now, as a full-time student, she works a part-time on-campus job as a Student Assistant at the BYU Alumni Office. Hannah is pursuing a career in recreational therapy, and she hopes to begin the Experience Design & Management program next year in BYU's prestigious Marriott School of Business.
